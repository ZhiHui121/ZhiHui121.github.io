PART 1

Author: Zhi Hui Date: December 22, 2024  22:40:28 Category: IELTS

1. Do you have any hobbies?

Yes, meditation is one of my main hobbies. I practice it daily in my free time because it helps me stay calm and manage my stress levels. I usually meditate for about 20 minutes in the morning, and I’ve found it really beneficial for my mental wellbeing.

2. Do you like swimming?

Yes, I love swimming. There’s something special about being surrounded by water - it makes me feel peaceful and weightless. When I’m in the pool, I can really relax and forget about my daily stress.

3. Is there an advertisement that made an impression on you when you were a child?

Yes, there’s a memorable phone advertisement from my childhood. It was for a music-enabled mobile phone, which was quite a luxury item back then since phones weren’t as common as they are now. The ad featured a beautiful woman listening to music on the phone. What impressed me most was the melodious background music and the artistic cinematography - the whole aesthetic was so elegant that it stayed with me for years.

4. Do you often feel bored?

Not really. I’m good at entertaining myself, so I rarely feel bored. I particularly enjoy reading all kinds of books, which keeps me occupied during my free time.

5. How important are friends to you?

Friends are very important to me because they give me a sense of belonging. I can share my feelings and opinions with them and get instant feedback, which makes me feel fulfilled. I especially value how they support me through both good times and difficult moments.

6. Do you often read books? When?

Yes, I’m passionate about reading. I particularly enjoy books that expand my knowledge, like biographies of great figures, as they teach me different perspectives and ways of thinking. I read whenever I have free time - it’s really become a daily habit for me.

7. Do you like the morning or evening?

I enjoy both times of day. In the morning, I feel energetic and my mind is clear - perfect for getting things done. In the evening, I love watching the sunset and its beautiful clouds, which brings me a sense of peace and happiness.

8. Where is your favorite place to sit?

One of my favorite places to sit is on the stones by the lake at the Summer Palace. From there, I can see the entire lake, which creates such a peaceful atmosphere. I love breathing in the fresh air and listening to the ducks - it’s incredibly refreshing and energizing.

9. In what conditions would you use a computer?

I use my computer daily since it’s essential for my work. I mainly use it for processing data, creating presentations, and watching videos. It’s my primary tool for both professional tasks and entertainment.

10. Have you ever seen old buildings in the city?

Beijing has many historic buildings. The most famous is the Forbidden City - the former imperial palace that’s 600 years old. It’s China’s capital city, containing not only royal buildings but also well-preserved traditional homes where ordinary citizens once lived.

11. Do you collect things?

I prefer a minimalist lifestyle, so I don’t usually collect things. I only keep what I need, which makes it easier to stay organized and maintain a clean space. This approach saves me time and energy on managing possessions.

12. Do you have a talent or something you are good at?

I’m quite sensitive to people’s emotions and atmosphere. I can often sense when tension is building, which helps me prevent conflicts before they happen. This emotional awareness has been really useful in both my personal and professional relationships.

13. Do you wear a watch?

Yes, I wear a smartwatch daily. It tracks my health data like heart rate, breathing patterns, and sleep quality. I particularly like its gentle vibration alarm in the morning - it’s much more pleasant than being startled awake by a loud alarm.

14. Did you enjoy traveling by car when you were a kid?

No, I didn’t enjoy car travel as a child. I was quite anxious about leaving familiar places, and I felt unsafe when traveling. Also, I would get motion sickness during long car rides, which made the experience quite unpleasant.

15. What will you do if you find something lost by others?

If I find something lost by others, I would take it to the nearest authority figure - like a security guard, manager, or information desk staff. I’d make sure to tell them exactly where and when I found the item so they can help return it to its owner.

16. What kind of websites do you often visit?

I frequently visit Reddit, a social news website where users share their experiences and opinions. I find the content valuable because people post about real-life situations and offer helpful perspectives. The discussions and stories often provide useful insights for my own life.

17. What do people usually buy at the street market?

At street markets, I’m often drawn to the fragrant fresh fruits and colorful clothing displays. Most people tend to buy fresh produce, street food, clothing, and daily necessities. These markets are popular for their affordable prices and variety.

18. What was your first mobile phone?

My first mobile phone was from a brand that’s no longer in business - I’ve forgotten which one exactly. But I clearly remember how excited I felt when I got my own phone for the first time.

19. How do you plan your time in a day?

I follow a daily routine. I wake up at 8 AM, get ready, and take the subway to work. After my 8-hour workday, I return home for various activities like reading, socializing, watching videos, and studying English. Then it’s bedtime.

20. Do you like watching sports programs on TV?

No, I’m not particularly interested in sports programs. I prefer watching shows that have engaging storylines or comedy elements. I find narrative-driven content more entertaining.

21. Do you like drawing?

While I’m not particularly skilled at drawing, I still enjoy it because it helps me relax and organize my thoughts. It’s like a form of meditation for me - even though my artwork isn’t perfect, the process itself is calming and therapeutic.

22. Do you like taking photos?

Yes, I really enjoy photography, both for myself and others. I love capturing beautiful moments in life. It’s especially rewarding when I take a good photo of someone else - seeing their happy reaction gives me a great sense of satisfaction.

23. Do you often send emails?

Here in China, I don’t send emails very often because most people prefer using WeChat for communication. Email is mainly used for work-related matters or international communication, but for daily contact, messaging apps are much more common.

24. Can you remember the dreams you had?

Yes, I usually remember my dreams quite well. Since I study psychology, I’ve become really interested in what my dreams mean. Every morning when I wake up, I try to recall what I dreamed about and sometimes write them down. It’s become a kind of daily habit for me.

25. Do you like looking at yourself in the mirror? How often?

Yes, I do! I look in the mirror quite often, probably around 10 times a day. I mainly use it to check my outfit and makeup, and make sure everything looks put together before heading out. It’s just become a natural part of my daily routine.

26. Do you like making friends?

Yes, absolutely! I really enjoy making friends and chatting with them - it’s a big source of happiness in my life. I love talking to different people because I always learn something new from them. I think it’s a great way to understand the world better. Actually, one reason I study English so hard is that I want to connect with more people and learn about their experiences.

27. Do you prefer to study at home or in a library?

Definitely the library! My study efficiency is much higher there compared to studying at home. At home, I often get distracted and end up sleeping or watching TikTok. Also, seeing so many other people studying hard at the library really motivates me - it creates this productive atmosphere that makes me want to focus on my work too

28. What is the view like around the place where you live?

Well, I live in a safe community with lots of greenery around. One of the best things is that there’s a subway station right at the entrance of our community, which makes transportation really convenient. The whole area is quite pleasant with all the plants and trees.

29. Do you enjoy watching interesting and funny movies?

Of course! Comedy is my favorite type of movie. These films always make me laugh and feel so much lighter while watching them. I think they’re a great way to relieve stress from work and daily life.

30. Do you like teamwork?

Yes, I really enjoy teamwork! I think it’s great because everyone can contribute their unique skills and energy to achieve something bigger than what any individual could do alone. Working in a team means we can share ideas and support each other to get better results.

31. Is fishing popular in your country?

Yes, fishing is quite popular in China, especially among middle-aged and elderly men. From what I’ve observed, they really enjoy it as a hobby. They get so excited when they catch a big fish and love showing it off to everyone around them!

32. Do you like perfume?

Yes, I do enjoy certain fragrances, especially citrus scents like orange. Pleasant scents can really lift my mood. However, I’m quite sensitive to most perfumes - they tend to make me feel dizzy, so I have to be selective about which ones I use.

33. Are the roads and streets in the area where you live busy?

No, not really. I live on the outskirts of the city, so there’s much less traffic and fewer people compared to downtown. It’s quite peaceful and quiet, which suits my lifestyle perfectly.”

34. Do you often carry coins with you?

No, I rarely do. In China, electronic payments like WeChat Pay and Alipay are so convenient that I hardly ever need to carry cash or coins. However, when I travel to countries like Thailand or Japan, I always make sure to carry coins since they’re much more commonly used there.

35. Do you have a favorite teacher?

Yes, my favorite teacher was my assistant supervisor during my postgraduate studies. She’s only about 8 years older than me and treats me like an elder sister. She’s given me many opportunities to learn and always guides me carefully. I really appreciate everything she’s done for me.

36. Did you have a bike when you were young?

Yes, my bicycle was an important part of my childhood. It was a special gift from my father, and I used it every day to ride to school. Later, when I grew older, I passed it on to my younger sister, who loved it just as much as I did.

37. What subject do you think is the most challenging at school?

When I was in school, physics was the most challenging subject for me. I found it really difficult because the symbols were too abstract, and I struggled to understand the concepts behind them

38. What did you enjoy doing as a child?

As a child, I really enjoyed playing with my friends, especially jumping rope with rubber bands. It’s a game where we would stretch rubber bands between our legs or chairs and take turns jumping over them. It was both challenging and fun because we had to work together, and we could create different patterns and heights to jump.

39. Are you a person who likes to stay at home?

Yes, I’m definitely a homebody. I’m quite comfortable spending time alone and can easily stay at home all day. There are lots of things I enjoy doing at home, like reading books, watching videos, and practicing meditation.

40. Where is your school?

My school is in Dalian, a beautiful coastal city in northeastern China. It’s about an hour’s flight from Beijing. The city is famous for its seafood and pleasant weather.

41. Have you ever tried any exciting activities?

Yes! When I traveled to Thailand, I tried snorkeling for the first time. I felt so excited because I could see all kinds of colorful fish swimming right in front of me. It was an amazing experience!

42. Which do you prefer, e-books or paper books?

I prefer paper books because I love the physical experience of reading them. When I hold a book, I can feel its weight and texture. I really enjoy the feeling and sound of turning pages - it helps me immerse myself in the world that the book describes.

43. Are you interested in news?

Yes, I’m very interested in news. Reading news helps me understand the world I live in better. I learn a lot from it, and it helps me make important decisions about my future, like career choices and which direction I should take in life.

44. When did you start using the Internet?

I started using the internet when I was 12 years old, after my father bought me my first computer. From then on, I began using it to search for information and explore different websites.

45. What do you usually eat for breakfast?

For breakfast, I usually start my day with a simple but nutritious meal. I typically have a slice of toasted bread with a fried egg, and I always accompany it with a cup of hot tea. I find this combination really energizing and it helps me stay focused throughout the morning.

46. Have you ever learned to play a musical instrument?

Yes, I have learned to play the guzheng, which is also known as the Chinese zither. It’s a beautiful traditional Chinese instrument with 21 strings. I started learning it when I was 12 years old. I particularly love its elegant and melodious sound - it can produce both gentle, flowing melodies and more dramatic, powerful pieces. It’s really a unique instrument that represents traditional Chinese culture.

47. What films do you like?

I’m a big fan of the Harry Potter series. These films really created a magical world that captured my imagination. I particularly love how they depict the fascinating world of wizardry, with all the spells, magical creatures, and the enchanting atmosphere of Hogwarts School. The movies do such an amazing job of bringing J.K. Rowling’s fantasy world to life. Even though they’re often categorized as children’s films, I think they appeal to people of all ages.

48. What languages can you speak?

I can speak Mandarin Chinese, which is my mother tongue. I can also communicate well in English, and I’ve picked up some basic Japanese. While I’m most confident in Mandarin, I really enjoy practicing my English and occasionally using simple Japanese phrases when I have the chance.

49. What city do you live in?

I live in Beijing, which is the capital city of China. It’s a fascinating city that perfectly blends modern development with rich historical heritage. One of its most remarkable features is the abundance of traditional architecture, such as the Forbidden City and the Summer Palace. These ancient imperial sites really showcase our traditional Chinese culture and history. While it’s true that Beijing is quite crowded, being one of the world’s largest cities, this also makes it an incredibly vibrant and dynamic place to live.

50. Is there anything that has made you feel happy lately?

Absolutely! I consider myself quite an energetic person, and I actually make a point of recording my happy moments. Recently, what made me particularly happy was giving a speech at an academic seminar. I received really positive feedback from the audience, which was very encouraging. It felt great to see that my presentation resonated with others and that my hard work paid off.

51. Do you wear T-shirts?

Yes, I absolutely love wearing T-shirts, especially those made of cotton. They’re really comfortable and versatile - you can dress them up or down depending on the occasion. I particularly enjoy how they come in so many different styles, colors, and designs. In fact, T-shirts make up a big part of my casual wardrobe, especially during the summer months.

52. Do you like weekends?

Yes, I absolutely love weekends! They’re my precious free time when I can take a break from work and study to do things I truly enjoy. I particularly love spending time outdoors in the garden, soaking up some sunshine, and practicing meditation to relax my mind. One of my favorite weekend activities is meeting up with friends for a meal, where we can catch up and have long, relaxed conversations. These simple pleasures really help me recharge for the week ahead.

53. What sports do you like?

I really enjoy swimming. There’s something so peaceful about being surrounded by water and gliding through it at my own pace. What I love most about swimming is that it feels almost meditative - when I’m in the water, I can clear my mind and feel completely relaxed. It’s not just great exercise for my body, but it’s also a wonderful way to find some inner peace.

54. What is your daily study routine?

My daily routine starts at 8 AM when I wake up. After getting dressed and freshening up with my morning routine, I have breakfast. Then I take the subway to my workplace. During the day, I focus on my work tasks and occasionally chat with colleagues during breaks. I usually finish work at 6 PM and head home. In the evening, I prepare dinner for myself and spend some time studying - either reading books or watching educational videos to improve my skills.

55. Do you like science?

Yes, I’m really fascinated by science. I love how it helps us understand the world around us by explaining complex phenomena in a clear and logical way. Thanks to scientific advances, we’ve developed amazing technologies that have transformed our daily lives. For example, we can now easily connect with people globally through smartphones and enjoy various conveniences that weren’t possible before.

56. Where did you go for your last holiday?

I went to Kyoto, Japan for my last holiday. It’s a beautiful traditional Japanese city filled with historic temples, peaceful gardens, and charming old streets. What I particularly loved about Kyoto was how well it preserves traditional Japanese culture and architecture. While I was there, I tried some amazing Japanese Wagyu beef, which was absolutely delicious. The whole experience gave me a real taste of authentic Japanese culture.

57. What would you do to relax?

To relax, I really enjoy taking slow walks in the garden where I can breathe in the fresh air and feel the sunshine on my skin. I find meditation particularly calming - it helps clear my mind and reduce stress. These simple activities might seem basic, but they really help me unwind and find peace after a busy day.

58. Are you good at math?

Well, to be honest, I’m not particularly good at mathematics. I often find it challenging because many mathematical concepts and proofs can be quite abstract and hard for me to grasp. While I can handle basic calculations, I tend to struggle with more complex mathematical problems. But I do admire people who are good at math and can understand these concepts easily.

59. Do you usually use a pen or a pencil?

Actually, since I started working, I mostly use my computer for writing and taking notes. While I used to write with pens and pencils more often during my school days, nowadays I find typing on a computer much more efficient for my work. It’s easier to edit, organize, and share digital documents. However, I still keep some pens on my desk for quick notes or signatures when needed.

60. Do you have anything to share with others recently?

Yes, actually I’ve been wanting to share something with my friends recently. I’ve started practicing mindfulness meditation, and it’s been really beneficial for me. It’s helped me reduce stress and stay more focused during my daily activities. I think it’s such a valuable practice that I want others to know about its benefits too.

61. Do you like to get up early in the morning?

No, I really don’t enjoy getting up early in the morning. Actually, it’s quite torturous for me since I’m not a morning person at all. I much prefer to wake up naturally according to my body clock, which usually means sleeping in a bit later. This way I feel much more refreshed and energetic throughout the day.

62. Do you keep plants at home?

No, I don’t keep any plants at home. I’m currently renting a place and living alone. Besides, I’m too busy to take care of plants properly - I would probably forget to water them regularly! I think plants need consistent care to stay healthy, which I just can’t provide right now.

63. What subjects are you studying?

I’m majoring in Psychology at university. I’m really fascinated by this subject because it helps me understand human behavior and mental processes. The courses are quite challenging but very interesting, especially when we learn about different psychological theories and conduct experiments.

64. When did you start using social media?

I started using social media when I was 12 years old, right after I got my first computer. The first platform I used was QQ, which is a popular social media app in China. I remember being really excited about adding my friends and chatting with them online for the first time.

65. Who do you live with?

Well, I’ve lived with different people at different stages of my life. I used to live with my parents when I was younger. Then during university, I shared a dormitory with my roommates. Now that I’m working, I live alone, which gives me more independence.

66. Have you learned anything about the history of your hometown?

Yes, I’ve learned quite a bit about my hometown, Dezhou, which is a city near Beijing. I enjoy reading local chronicles, and I remember finding an interesting old brochure about the city when I was young. From these materials, I learned that Dezhou has historically been an important transportation hub, connecting different parts of China.

67. Are the people in your neighborhood nice and friendly?

Yes, I’m lucky to have very friendly neighbors. Most of them are around my age, which makes it easy for us to get along. They’re really nice people - they often share cherries from their garden with me and are always willing to help out when I need it.
